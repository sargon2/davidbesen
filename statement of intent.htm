It is fitting that this essay be called a Statement of Intent, since intent is precisely what I was lacking the first time I went to Colorado State University.  I had no direction, no motivation, and no understanding of my purpose or place at Colorado State.  As a consequence I was unable to care about my studies, and unable to apply myself to them.

After leaving the university on uncomfortable terms, I began a career in computer science.  I was quite happy with my work and, over time, I advanced to head of the Information Technology department of a small market research firm (see included resume). 

Despite my job accomplishments, I wanted something more in my life.  I began to cultivate my desire to learn about the world around me.  Naturally, the more I learned the more I wanted to learn, and soon I turned my sights to the best resource available for self-improvement – college.  I truly enjoyed my IT work, so my motivations for returning to school were not financial or career-advancing in nature; life experience had taught me that self improvement is what’s important.  I left work to become a full-time student at Front Range Community College.

At FRCC, I enjoyed my classes more than I had ever thought was possible.  Subjects such as history and English composition that had never before appealed to me were now deeply interesting.  I earned a 4.0 GPA my first semester back in school, and I enrolled again for a full class load next semester (fall 2005).

I would like to return to Colorado State in spring 2006 and complete my original educational plan of getting a B.S. in Computer Science.  To aid in this, I hope to qualify for the Fresh Start program.  After completing my bachelor’s degree, I will strongly consider graduate school.  Also, since I’ve been studying conversational Japanese on my own, I plan to enroll in Japanese language courses for the duration of my return.

Someone once said that we are defined more by our failures than by our successes, and in my case this is quite true.  In failing my first attempt at college, I learned more about myself than I would have otherwise; now that I’ve at last truly begun the lifelong task of learning, I believe I will be able to better make use of what Colorado State University has to offer.
